Kellgren-Lawrence Classification System

Standard radiographic grading system for osteoarthritis severity assessment

The Kellgren-Lawrence (KL) grading system is the gold standard for radiographic assessment of knee osteoarthritis severity. This classification scheme evaluates the presence and extent of osteophytes, joint space narrowing, subchondral sclerosis, and deformity visible on plain radiographs. The system categorizes osteoarthritis progression into five distinct grades, from healthy joints (Grade 0) to severe osteoarthritis (Grade 4).

Grade Classification Radiographic Findings
Grade 0 Normal No radiographic features of osteoarthritis. Normal joint space and bone structure.
Grade 1 Doubtful Questionable joint space narrowing. Possible osteophytic lipping. Minimal changes.
Grade 2 Mild Definite osteophytes. Possible joint space narrowing on anteroposterior weight-bearing radiograph.
Grade 3 Moderate Multiple osteophytes. Definite joint space narrowing. Sclerosis. Possible attrition of bone.
Grade 4 Severe Large osteophytes. Marked joint space narrowing. Severe sclerosis. Definite bony deformity.

About This System

This automated classification system utilizes deep convolutional neural networks (CNN) trained on radiographic data to provide Kellgren-Lawrence grading assessments. The model employs advanced computer vision techniques for feature extraction and classification.
